Chemistry Trends in the Periodic Table

1. Ionization Enthalpy (Ionization Energy)

Definition: Ionization enthalpy is the amount of energy required to remove an electron from an isolated gaseous atom in its ground state.

Trends in the Periodic Table:

  • Across a Period: Ionization enthalpy increases from left to right across a period. This is because the nuclear charge increases, which attracts the electrons more strongly, making it harder to remove an electron.
  • Down a Group: Ionization enthalpy decreases down a group. This is due to the increase in atomic size and the shielding effect, which makes it easier to remove an outer electron.

2. Metallic Character

Definition: Metallic character refers to the set of properties associated with metals, including the ability to conduct electricity, malleability, ductility, and the tendency to lose electrons and form positive ions.

Trends in the Periodic Table:

  • Across a Period: Metallic character decreases from left to right across a period. As you move from metals to non-metals, the tendency to lose electrons decreases.
  • Down a Group: Metallic character increases down a group. The outer electrons are further from the nucleus and are more easily lost, making the elements more metallic.

3. Electron Gain Enthalpy (Electron Affinity)

Definition: Electron gain enthalpy is the amount of energy released when an electron is added to a neutral atom in the gaseous state to form a negative ion.

Trends in the Periodic Table:

  • Across a Period: Electron gain enthalpy generally becomes more negative (more energy is released) from left to right across a period, especially for non-metals, as they have a greater tendency to gain electrons.
  • Down a Group: Electron gain enthalpy becomes less negative (less energy is released) down a group. This is because the added electron is further from the nucleus and experiences more shielding, making it less stable.
